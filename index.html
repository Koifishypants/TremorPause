How to Fix the Severity Discrepancy
Step 1 — Open "Feature Debug" on the connected hand and tremor your hand. The panel shows every intermediate value. Look for:

energy value — the model's root splits at 622.51. If your energy is far below that (e.g. 5, 50, 200), your data is in a different unit than training data (likely m/s² vs raw ADC counts).
If energy is above 622 → the issue is in raw_prob or freq_weight.

raw_prob — this is what the Random Forest outputs before the sigmoid. If this is ~0.08 regardless of tremor, the features don't match training data scale.

freq_weight — sigmoid output. Should be > 0.7 for tremor. If it's 0.01–0.1, the dominant frequency is much lower than your threshold (ft). Open Settings and paste the Hz value printed by Python's get_optimal_hz_via_j_index().

Step 2 — Check the BLE log (first Raw[1]–Raw[5] lines)
These show the raw X,Y,Z values from the Arduino. If they're small floats (0–10), Arduino sends m/s² or g. If they're large integers (0–1023 or similar), it sends raw ADC. The training data scale must match these values for the model to work.
